I want to…

…report a problem

Do so normally, on the project's issue tracker, or as detailed in their documentation contribution guidelines.

…contribute some code, docs, art…

Do so normally, by submitting a pull request. Make sure to follow the project's contribution guidelines!

…get features, fixes, etc released

Wait for the project's caretakers to do so. They can't be rushed, and will do it at their pace. That might be days, weeks, months, or even years, but you won't have to do anything.

Request to become a temporary release manager. You will be responsible for getting the release ready and publishing it, and you will be given rights to do it. Follow the release request process.

…become a permanent caretaker

As a prerequisite, you should ensure you know what you're getting into by reading this website (start below) and the project's own documentation, guidelines, and culture. Once you're ready, follow the caretaker request process.

…want to use this for my own work

Include a blurb in your readme explaining that you use the Caretaker Model, with a link to this website. And of course apply the processes and principles! You may also want to set up automation.
